Southlake Focus Group

Who We Are

The Southlake Focus Group (SFG) is a career transition-networking group designed to help people get back into the workforce as soon as possible.

We are a local community resource that is 100% VOLUNTEER and we provide our services at NO COST to either members or employers

We are Independent in Affiliation and Interdenominational in reach with 5,000+ Members since our founding in 2000.

We serve all of DFW and have regular attendees from Dallas, Collin, Denton and Tarrant counties.

We exchange job leads, network, offer useful job search strategies, share information on career workshops and provide support, motivation and encouragement to each other.

Brief History

The Southlake Focus Group started with 4 people meeting at the Corner Bakery (Southlake Town Square). We out grew the Corner Bakery in 2002 and migrated to the White's Chapel Methodist Church (Southlake). We out grew that facility in 2003 and moved to a larger meeting space with the First Baptist Church (Keller). Construction at First Baptist Keller began in 2008, which coincided with completed construction at White's Chapel. Our group returned to White's Chapel in 2008.

An SFG Alumni Group was formed to continue networking and support the Southlake Focus Group. "Pay It Forward" has been the common thread bonding the two groups known as the best networking groups in the Dallas / Fort Worth Region.

SPECIAL NOTICE: For your first visit, plan to arrive 15 minutes early. You will be welcomed by one of our Volunteers who will explain briefly what to expect on your first meeting and also to meet a few of the members to get acquainted quickly. You will take part of the opening meeting and then be taken to an Orientation Session during which you learn how the meetings are organized.
